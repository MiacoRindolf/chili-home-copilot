fix(crypto+queue): round-13 asset-aware pause + crypto exit hardening

User flagged that the round-12 market-hours pause unfairly blocked
crypto patterns (which trade 24/7) and asked to look at crypto exits
generally. Three concrete improvements:

1. Asset-aware market-hours pause (`learning.py`)
   Round 12 paused ALL backtests Mon-Fri 9:30 AM-4:00 PM ET because
   throughput collapsed during US regular hours due to upstream-API
   contention. But the contention is with STOCK trading systems --
   crypto runs 24/7 on Coinbase / Robinhood-crypto endpoints which
   are NOT contended during stock-market hours.
   Fix: filter the batch to drop ONLY stock-only patterns
   (asset_class IN ('stocks','stock')) during market hours. Crypto
   ('crypto') and unspecified ('all', NULL) keep running. Cycle
   only fully pauses when 100% of the batch is stock-only.

2. Crypto exit broker-qty clamp (`crypto/exit_monitor.py`)
   The A-5 clamp from round 1 explicitly skipped crypto. Now applied
   symmetrically: before submitting place_crypto_sell_order, look up
   the broker's actual position via get_crypto_positions(). If broker
   holds 0 (already sold externally), mark as no_position. If broker
   holds less than local Trade.quantity, clamp the sell qty. If the
   broker call fails (qty unknown), defer per the no-hardcoded-fallback
   rule -- never silently widen.

3. Implausible-quote rejection (`crypto/exit_monitor._evaluate_exit_triggers`)
   Found in audit: trade 585 ARB-USD was stopped at
   `px=0.00075706 <= stop=0.110331` -- the upstream quote returned a
   value 145x lower than reality (data error). The stop trigger fired
   on garbage data and sold the position at the real -4.20 PnL.
   Fix: reject any quote where px/entry > 10 or < 0.1. Returns
   'no_trigger:implausible_quote' so the next pass can retry with a
   fresh quote rather than acting on a corrupted one. When entry is
   unset (0/None), skip the guard since we can't judge plausibility.
