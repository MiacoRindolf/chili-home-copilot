9. Conclusion 
 
This study investigates the presence of intraday momentum and return predictability in the  
Swedish financial market, focusing on the OMXS30 index and its selected constituent stocks.  
The findings reveal that while there is some evidence of intraday momentum in individual  
stocks such as SBB and Sinch, the overall effect is weak compared to larger markets like US  
and China, specifically when comparing index to index such as OMXS30, Shanghai Composite 
(Zhang et al. 2019) and S&P 500 (Gao et al. 2018). Despite mixed results, this study contributes 
to the existing literature by providing insights to the dynamics of intraday returns in the Swedish 
market. The findings indicate that while intraday momentum exists, it is not strong or consistent 
enough to form the basis for reliable trading strategies across all stocks.  
 
The study’s limitations include the short time -period and focus on a single market. Future  
research could extend the analysis to longer timeframes, incorporate additional stocks, and  
explore other market. Investigating the first half-hour of the trading day using minute -by-
minute data, particularly during periods of high volume and volatility, could provide further  
insights. Additionally, developing sentiment analysis algorithms to evaluate the impact of news 
on intraday returns could enhance the predictability of trading strategies.
