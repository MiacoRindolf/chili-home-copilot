Abstract 
This study examines intraday momentum and return predictability in the Swedish financial 
market, focusing on the OMX Stockholm 30 index (OMXS30) and selected constituent stocks: 
ABB, Investor B, Nordea SE, SBB B and Sinch. Utilizing high-frequency data from 2 March 
2023 to 29th of February 2024, investigating whether the first half-hour returns, and the second-
to-last half-hour returns can predict the last half-hour returns. Despite some evidence of intraday 
momentum in individual stocks like SBB and Sinch, the overall findings indicate weak 
predictability compared to larger markets such as the U.S (Gao et al. 2018). and China (Zhang 
et al. 2019). This outcome is discussed in the context of the theories of late-informed investors 
and infrequent rebalancing, suggesting that these phenomena may contribute to the observed 
patterns, but are not sufficiently strong to support consistent trading strategies.
