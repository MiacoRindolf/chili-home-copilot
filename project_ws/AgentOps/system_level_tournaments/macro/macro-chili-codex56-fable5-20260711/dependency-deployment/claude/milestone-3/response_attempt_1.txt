**What the change does**

- **`app/graph.py`** — adds `plan_fingerprint(services)`: a SHA-256 hex digest of the canonical dependency graph (each service name paired with its sorted, de-duplicated dependencies, sorted by name). It's deterministic across service order, dependency order, and processes (no salted `hash()`), changes whenever the graph itself changes, and validates via `dependency_waves` so invalid graphs still raise `ValueError("invalid deployment graph")`. `dependency_waves` is untouched.
- **`app/state.py`** — `DeploymentState` becomes durable/rehydratable: `__init__(fingerprint=None, completed_waves=(), deployed=(), failed=(), rolled_back=(), waves=())` (so `DeploymentState()` still works exactly as in milestone 2, and `DeploymentState(**state.snapshot())` round-trips), plus a `record_completed_wave(index)` method and `fingerprint`/`completed_waves` in `snapshot()`.
- **`app/executor.py`** — `execute_deployment` keeps its signature and plan-only/rollback semantics, and now: fails closed with `ValueError("deployment plan changed")` before any deploy call or state mutation when the state's fingerprint belongs to another graph (fingerprint is adopted onto fresh states); skips waves the state already marks completed; exposes `resumed` and `completed_waves` (cumulative, sorted tuple) on every result path; and on an unhealthy wave rolls back **prior deployed services from the state** as well, in strict newest-first reverse order (milestone-2 stateless rollback order is unchanged).
