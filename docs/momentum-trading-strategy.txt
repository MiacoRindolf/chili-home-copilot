CHILI Momentum Day Trading Strategy
====================================

This document describes CHILI's momentum day trading approach. The initial
framework below was seeded from professional day trading best practices,
but CHILI does NOT treat any of these rules as gospel. Every assumption
listed here is a starting hypothesis that CHILI's brain continuously
tests against real market data via its self-validation system.

Each learning cycle, CHILI:
- Tests every key hypothesis (e.g. "MACD negative = bad") against actual
  historical returns and win rates.
- If the data contradicts an assumption, CHILI weakens its weight and
  logs a "hypothesis challenged" insight.
- If the data confirms an assumption, CHILI reinforces it.
- CHILI also explores novel patterns that no strategy taught it —
  discovering its own edge in the market.
- All scoring weights are adaptive: they start at defaults but evolve
  based on what CHILI's brain learns from real data.

The goal is not to replicate any single trader's style, but to absorb
the best ideas, stress-test them, and ultimately build something better.


1. THE FIVE PILLARS OF STOCK SELECTION
--------------------------------------

Every momentum candidate must pass all five checks before consideration:

1. **Float**: Prefer stocks with a float under 20 million shares. Micro-floats
   (under 5M shares) move the fastest because limited supply means any buying
   pressure creates outsized price moves. Above 50M shares, momentum trades
   become much harder.

2. **Relative Volume**: The stock must be trading at least 3x its 20-day
   average volume (5x or higher is ideal). High relative volume means the
   institutional and retail crowd is paying attention *right now*.

3. **Price Range**: The sweet spot is $2 to $20 for small-account traders.
   Below $1 is penny-stock territory (risky, illiquid). Above $50, the
   percentage moves shrink unless the catalyst is massive.

4. **Catalyst / Gap**: The stock should have a clear reason for moving —
   earnings surprise, FDA approval, partnership, sector rotation, or a
   pre-market gap of 5%+ from the prior close. No-catalyst moves fade.

5. **Chart Pattern**: The best entries come on the *first pullback* after
   the initial breakout. The pullback should be clean (fewer than 5 red
   candles, decreasing volume on the pullback) and occur near a support
   level (VWAP, EMA-9, or prior resistance turned support).


2. MACD GATING RULES
---------------------

MACD is the primary momentum gatekeeper. These rules are non-negotiable:

- **MACD positive + histogram rising**: GREEN LIGHT. The trend is
  accelerating. This is the ideal entry condition.

- **MACD positive + histogram flat/declining**: YELLOW. Momentum is
  slowing but the trend is still intact. Only enter on perfect pullback
  setups with excellent risk:reward.

- **MACD negative (below signal line) + histogram below zero**: RED.
  Never enter a new long position when MACD has crossed below its signal
  line. This is the single most important rule. If you're already in the
  trade, this is your exit signal.

- **MACD crossover from below**: Watch for potential new entry, but wait
  for confirmation (histogram turns positive for 2+ bars).


3. PULLBACK ENTRY CRITERIA
---------------------------

The "bread and butter" trade is the first pullback after a breakout:

a) Stock gaps up or breaks out on volume (5x+ relative volume ideal)
b) Stock makes a new high on the timeframe
c) Stock pulls back 3-8% from the high
d) Pullback has 2-4 red candles with *decreasing* volume
e) MACD remains positive throughout the pullback
f) Entry trigger: first green candle on the pullback reversal

Avoid:
- Pullbacks with 7 or more consecutive red candles (setup is dead)
- Pullbacks where MACD flips negative (momentum is lost)
- Pullbacks on *increasing* volume (selling pressure, not just rest)


4. VOLUME PROFILE ANALYSIS
---------------------------

Volume tells the truth about who's in control:

- **Green candle volume > red candle volume** (over the last 10 bars):
  Buyers are stronger. This confirms the trend.

- **Red candle volume > green candle volume**: Sellers are stronger.
  The move may be exhausting. Be cautious.

- **Volume top / topping tail**: If the last candle has an upper wick
  that is more than 2x the body size AND this happens on high volume,
  it signals a rejection / reversal. This is a key *avoidance* signal.
  Sellers overwhelmed buyers at the highs.

- **Volume expansion on breakout**: The breakout candle should have
  at least 2-3x the recent average volume. Without volume confirmation,
  the breakout is likely to fail.


5. RISK MANAGEMENT
------------------

- **Risk:Reward minimum**: 2:1 (risk $1 to make $2). The scanner
  requires at least 2:1 for "immaculate" classification.

- **Position sizing**: Never risk more than 2% of account on a single
  trade. For a $25,000 account, max loss per trade is $500.

- **Daily target**: Aim for 1-2% of account value per day. Compounding
  small consistent wins beats chasing home runs.

- **Daily max loss**: Stop trading for the day after losing 3% of
  account value. Revenge trading is the #1 account killer.

- **Stop loss placement**: Place stops just below the pullback low or
  1.5x ATR below entry. Never average down on a losing trade.

- **Profit taking**: Take 1/3 off at 1:1, move stop to break-even,
  take another 1/3 at 2:1, let the rest ride with a trailing stop.


6. TIME-OF-DAY RULES
---------------------

- **9:30-10:30 AM ET**: The power hour. This is when momentum is
  strongest. 70%+ of the best day trades happen in this window.
  The scanner prioritizes this period.

- **10:30-11:30 AM**: Still viable for first pullback entries, but
  the initial momentum wave has often played out.

- **11:30 AM - 2:00 PM**: The "chop zone." Most moves have exhausted.
  Avoid new entries unless a strong catalyst develops.

- **2:00-4:00 PM**: Can see a late-day push or reversal. Be cautious;
  this window is more suitable for closing positions than opening them.


7. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
----------------------------

1. Entering when MACD is negative (the #1 mistake)
2. Chasing a stock that's already up 20%+ without a pullback
3. Ignoring volume tops / topping tails
4. Trading stocks with floats above 50M (they don't move enough)
5. Not having a stop loss before entering
6. Trading during the midday chop zone
7. Entering on extended pullbacks (7+ red candles)
8. Averaging down on a losing position
9. Ignoring the risk:reward ratio (must be >= 2:1)
10. Trading without a catalyst or clear reason for the move


8. THE "IMMACULATE" SETUP
--------------------------

CHILI's scanner flags an "immaculate" trade when ALL of these align:

- Intraday score >= 8.0/10
- MACD positive with rising histogram
- Relative volume >= 3x (5x+ preferred)
- Clean pullback (< 5 red candles, decreasing volume)
- Risk:Reward >= 2:1
- Favorable time of day (ideally 9:30-10:30 AM ET)

When the scanner finds an immaculate setup, it sends an immediate alert.
These are rare (0-3 per day) but represent the highest-probability
trades the system can identify.

Note: Even these thresholds (8.0 score, 3x vol, 2:1 R:R) are adaptive.
If CHILI's brain learns that 7.5-score setups with 4x volume consistently
outperform 8.0-score setups with 3x volume, it will adjust accordingly.


9. NEWS SENTIMENT INTEGRATION
-------------------------------

CHILI enriches every market snapshot with news sentiment data, adding a
qualitative dimension to its purely technical analysis:

a) **VADER Sentiment Scoring**: Each ticker's recent news headlines are
   scored using the VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner)
   NLP model. Headlines receive a compound score from -1.0 (very bearish)
   to +1.0 (very bullish), with labels: bullish (>=0.15), bearish (<=-0.15),
   or neutral.

b) **Aggregated News Sentiment**: For each snapshot, CHILI computes the
   average sentiment score across all recent headlines and stores:
   - news_sentiment (float): average compound score
   - news_count (int): number of headlines analyzed

c) **News-Based Patterns**: The brain mines for combined technical +
   sentiment patterns, including:
   - Bullish news + RSI oversold (<35) — contrarian catalyst bounce
   - Bearish news + RSI overbought (>70) — sell signal confluence
   - Bullish news + MACD positive + EMA stack — momentum confirmation
   - Bearish news + MACD negative — downtrend confirmation
   - High news volume (5+) + high trading volume (2x) — event-driven breakout
   - Strong bullish news + stochastic oversold — high-probability bounce
   - Neutral news + strong trend (ADX>30) + RSI<40 — trend pullback, no fear

d) **ML Feature Integration**: News sentiment and news count are fed as
   features into the GradientBoosting ML model alongside technical indicators,
   giving the predictor a richer view of the market state.


10. FUNDAMENTAL DATA INTEGRATION
----------------------------------

CHILI supplements technical and sentiment data with key fundamentals:

a) **Stored Per Snapshot**: Each market snapshot records:
   - pe_ratio (float): trailing P/E ratio
   - market_cap_b (float): market cap in billions

b) **ML Features**: P/E ratio is log-transformed (to handle wide ranges)
   and included as a feature in the GradientBoosting model.

c) **AI Context**: When running AI Analyze, CHILI assembles a rich
   fundamentals section including P/E (trailing + forward), EPS, PEG,
   P/S, P/B, EV/EBITDA, revenue + growth, margins, FCF, D/E, ROE, and
   dividend yield — giving the LLM full fundamental context for its
   verdict alongside technical indicators.


11. HOW CHILI EVOLVES BEYOND THIS
----------------------------------

This strategy document represents CHILI's starting knowledge. Here's how
the brain improves over time:

a) **Hypothesis Testing**: Every learning cycle, CHILI explicitly tests
   assumptions like "MACD positive always outperforms MACD negative" by
   comparing actual 5-day and 10-day returns across both groups. If an
   assumption fails, CHILI creates a "hypothesis challenged" insight and
   reduces the weight of that factor in scoring.

b) **Novel Pattern Discovery**: CHILI doesn't just test existing rules —
   it explores combinations that no human taught it. For example, it might
   discover that "Bollinger Band oversold + MACD negative" is actually a
   profitable contrarian bounce setup, even though conventional wisdom
   says to avoid MACD-negative entries. It also discovers news+technical
   combinations that no trader has explicitly formalized.

c) **Adaptive Weight Evolution**: All scoring weights (MACD bonus, float
   bonus, volume thresholds, etc.) start at defaults but continuously
   adjust based on the brain's confidence in related patterns. High
   confidence in a pattern = stronger weight. Low confidence = weakened
   weight. Weights are bounded so they can't swing too wildly.

d) **Cross-Validation**: Discovered patterns are backtested across
   multiple tickers and timeframes. Patterns that only work on a few
   stocks get demoted. Patterns that work broadly get promoted.

e) **Decay & Demotion**: Patterns that haven't been seen in 30+ days
   gradually lose confidence. Patterns with lots of evidence but low
   win rates are deactivated. The brain prunes what doesn't work.

f) **Multi-Signal Confluence**: CHILI combines technical indicators,
   news sentiment, and fundamental data to form richer hypotheses.
   A bullish pattern backed by positive sentiment and strong fundamentals
   receives higher conviction than one standing on technicals alone.

The result: CHILI starts with a solid foundation but continuously tests,
challenges, and improves every aspect of its trading logic. No single
strategy or teacher limits what it can become.
