## Findings — what I got wrong, what's actually true

### My framing error

The brief said: *"validation residuals are consistently positive (realized return > miner mean) → miner is underpredicting → most likely Hypothesis A or B (entry-time bias or horizon mismatch)."*

Wrong on two counts:
- `realized_validation_residual` is `mean(|residual|)` — direction-blind by construction.
- The 13-40 bps numbers I reported were magnitudes, not signed offsets. I read the absolute values as if they had direction.

Claude Code caught this in the report's "Important framing correction" section. Without that catch, I'd have queued the next brief on the same wrong premise.

**Lesson for future briefs:** verify the data-type semantics of any column I cite before reasoning about direction. `mean(...)` in column names usually means "average of N values"; `_residual` could mean signed or absolute error depending on the producer.

### Hypothesis B is the primary driver, and it's not a "small fix"

The 1800s mapped bucket has actual hold times of 647s–2696s. **The miner records price at exactly 1800s after fire; the realized exit happens anywhere in a 4x-wide window.** For volatile crypto pairs, that easily explains 30 bps disagreement.

**This isn't a hygiene fix — it's a measurement-model decision.** Three plausible approaches, each with tradeoffs:

1. **Per-exit forward-return** (Option 1): when an exit lands, look up `fast_orderbook` at `fired_at + actual_holding_period_s` and compute realized-equivalent forward return. **Clean. ~50-100 LOC. Operationally cheap (one DB read per exit).**
2. **Continuous forward-return curve**: store forward-return at multiple sub-horizon timestamps. More accurate, but adds 5-10x observation volume to `fast_signal_decay`.
3. **Reframe residual semantics**: redefine "horizon" in the miner as a measurement timestamp, not a prediction; always validate hold-time-matched.

Claude Code prefers Option 1. I agree — Option 1 is the smallest scope with the cleanest semantic alignment. Option 2 trades accuracy for storage; Option 3 is the right *eventual* answer but requires touching every consumer.

### Hypothesis C is now fixed for DOGE specifically

Pre-fix, miner used `(best_bid + best_ask) / 2`. Exit-side uses `best_bid` for long close. Half-spread offset = 45 bps for DOGE, ~0 bps for BTC.

**Per-ticker correction expected** as new observations accumulate (existing residuals stick because Welford means converge slowly). Verification at the next eval will see DOGE residuals drop ~45 bps; BTC/ETH/SOL residuals stable.

**The fix doesn't help BTC+SOL (F8b's targets) materially.** It does mean DOGE's measurement is now more honest — which matters for any future signal that includes DOGE.
