## Quant / Math Notes

Sizing floor:

- Signal: active soft derater multipliers, base risk, setup alias, viability score, frontside score, hard blocker multipliers.
- Model: floor fraction = geometric mean of active soft multipliers, falling back to the configured documented fraction when no soft multipliers are active.
- Why causal: correlated soft warnings (midday, hot/cold, daily room, fatigue) often describe the same risk regime; multiplying them assumes independence and over-penalizes quality setups. Geometric mean treats them as overlapping evidence.
- Failure safe: hard blockers still win; non-equity, unknown alias, low viability, weak frontside, red/halt/extreme/liquidity breakers do not get lifted.

Scheduler replay:

- Signal: venue availability, execution family, terminal/expired state, tick-arm state, quality score, urgency from age/expiry, expected PnL when known.
- Model: cheap prefilter removes rows that cannot legally run before slot accounting; available rows rank by self-normalized quality/urgency/tick-arm evidence inside budget. The live scheduler cap is derived from configured watch fanout, open-position ceiling, and entry concurrency in decoupled mode; legacy mode keeps the historical 15 useful-session cap. The timeline layer replays multiple scheduler ticks and counts expected PnL as missed only when a candidate terminalizes/expires without selection, so a budget-delayed candidate selected later is not falsely marked as missed.
- Why causal: capacity should model broker/risk calls and order-submission bandwidth, not rows that cannot submit.
- Failure safe: unavailable/wrong-venue rows are skipped/terminalizable with reasons; eligible equity rows behind them still get evaluated; budget remains bounded.

Event-driven admission:

- Signal: IQFeed/WS tick notification, Ross small-cap proof, evidence/cooldown, immediate session tick count.
- Model: market data event creates watcher and immediately ticks it; scheduler remains fallback/maintenance only.
- Why causal: Ross scalp edge often lives in seconds; a 10s polling loop misses CANF/JEM-class windows.
- Failure safe: Ross universe proof, cooldown, operator/broker/risk gates and structural stop remain at admission/entry.

Wait coverage:

- Signal: emitted `waiting_for_*` / Ross starter wait reason plus pullback high/low.
- Model: every actionable wait reason emitted by `entry_gates.py` must be in the tick-arm allowlist; tape-hold is an explicit subset for valid pullback/reclaim holds. Setup trace records whether the source wait was tick-armed, tape-hold eligible, and had both pullback levels.
- Why causal: if a Ross setup is waiting on an actionable level, the runner must either arm that level at tick speed or clearly explain why it remained candle/scheduler-bound.
- Failure safe: this does not force an entry; it only surfaces eligibility and prevents new wait strings from bypassing tick-arm coverage silently.

Daily overhead-room sizing:

- Signal: signed daily ATR distance to the 200SMA plus the nearest prior daily swing/gap resistance above entry.
- Model: `dist_to_sma_200_atr = (price - sma200) / daily_atr`; negative means the 200SMA is overhead, positive means price has cleared it. The sizing multiplier uses the nearest binding overhead distance in ATR units and ramps from `daily_room_size_floor` to `1.0` with smoothstep over `daily_room_band_atr`.
- Why causal: Ross daily-room guidance is about supply above the entry, not the raw dollar distance. ATR normalization makes the rule comparable across low-priced names and avoids magic cent thresholds.
- Failure safe: this is a soft size-down only; no daily context or no overhead distance returns `1.0`. The optional overhead veto and blue-sky detector remain explicit flags; the daily context module is now present in the deployed runtime.

Float-turnover over-rotation sizing:

- Signal: persisted Ross day volume, float shares, current session elapsed fraction, and RVOL pace.
- Model: `rotations = day_volume / float_shares`; `pressure = rotations * session_elapsed_fraction / sqrt(max(rvol_pace, 1))`; `mult = floor + (1 - floor) / (1 + pressure)`.
- Why causal: early low-float volume can be ignition, while late repeated float turnover can mean the easy supply/demand imbalance is crowded or exhausted. Session fraction makes the same raw rotations less punitive early and more punitive late; RVOL pace mitigates when the turnover is genuinely exceptional for the current clock rather than stale churn.
- Failure safe: missing float, day volume, session time, or disabled flag returns `1.0`. It is a sizing multiplier only, participates in the A-setup soft-derater floor, and never becomes a hard no-trade gate.

Setup-trace audit:

- Signal: event payload `setup_trace` or older top-level setup fields.
- Model: pure scanner over event-shaped rows plus a read-only recent-DB wrapper; reports missing structural-stop coverage, missing A-setup floor coverage, structural setups without stop levels, wait reasons that did not tick-arm, tape-hold wait mismatches, and wait reasons missing pullback levels.
- Why causal: the live incidents were not only bad gates; they were also blind spots. This converts "we need to inspect logs" into a repeatable post-deploy telemetry invariant.
- Failure safe: read-only and broker-free; it cannot place orders or mutate sessions.
